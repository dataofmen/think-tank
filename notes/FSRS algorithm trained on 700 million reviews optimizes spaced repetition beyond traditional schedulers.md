FSRS (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler) is an open-source algorithm trained on over 700 million review records from the Anki community. It replaces Anki's legacy SM-2 scheduler with a machine-learning-based approach that adapts interval scheduling to individual learning patterns.

The key advantage over traditional schedulers is data scale: SM-2 was designed from small-scale experiments, while FSRS leverages the collective review history of millions of users. Users can set a target retention rate (e.g., 0.90) and FSRS calculates optimal review intervals to hit that target with minimal total reviews.

This represents a broader pattern where community-scale data enables algorithms that outperform expert-designed heuristics.
